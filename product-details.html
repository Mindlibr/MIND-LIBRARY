Laut Bercerita

Circulation Services

Laut Bercerita is a novel by an Indonesian writer named Leila Salikha Chudori. She is also a journalist at Tempo magazine. This novel, published in 2017, raises the themes of friendship, romance, family, and loss. Set in the 90s and 2000s, this novel is able to captivate its readers to break through the space of the past and look back at the events that occurred in the year in question.

Laut Bercerita tells about the cruelty and brutality experienced by student activist groups during the New Order. Not only that, this novel also reflects on the disappearance of 13 activists, even until now no one has received any clues.

The story in the novel Laut Bercerita is divided into two parts with a very different time interval. The first part is told from the perspective of a character named Biru Laut and his fellow activists as they complete their vision or goals. While in the second part, the story is taken from the perspective of Asmara Jati, Laut's younger brother who has goals or visions that tend to be different from Laut's.

Helen Li "Mixed. This is not just a sad story. Everything is very complex. At the time of the 98 incident I was still too young and did not really understand what was happening, but through the depiction that Laut experienced I was brought back to that time with myself now. How tense, scared and enthusiastic the young people were to free their own country even though in the end they had to die at the hands of what people said but they were really demons!! But the most emotionally draining was chapter 2. As a family, a friend who lives in question marks, grieving in uncertainty .. only with words all that explains the existing distress."
