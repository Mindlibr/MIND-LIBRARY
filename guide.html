1. Book Review

A book review is an in-depth discussion of a selected book with participants. The book is usually chosen for its educational value, inspiration, or relevance to a certain issue.

Main objectives:

  • Explore the content and message of the book
  • Encourage critical thinking among participants
  • Promote discussion and idea exchange

This activity may involve the author, active readers, or scholars as speakers.

2. Training

Training refers to learning activities aimed at improving specific knowledge or skills. In the context of libraries or literacy, training can include:

  • Creative writing workshops
  • Digital content creation training
  • Speed reading sessions
  • Information literacy training

Purpose: to equip participants with practical knowledge applicable in school, college, or everyday life.

3. Storytelling

Storytelling is a form of narrative sharing, often using expressions, voice modulation, or visual aids such as pictures and puppets. While ideal for children, it can be adapted for all age groups depending on the story.

Benefits of storytelling:

  • Enhances imagination and creativity
  • Instills moral or cultural values
  • Promotes reading interest through engaging stories

4. Talk Show

A talk show is an informal yet insightful discussion that features speakers from various fieldsâ€”authors, public figures, educators, or professionals. It is usually moderated and concludes with a Q&A session.

Objectives:

  • Inspire and share new insights
  • Present specific themes in an engaging way
  • Create space for interaction between audience and speakers
